Our Mission

  • TN Rao School for Girls endeavours to prepare each student to enable them to develop intellectual powers as well as strength of culture, morality and spirituality.

Our Philosophy

  • to guide students toward intellectual, artistic, physical, technological, and social competence.
  • to spark passion. We want children to become deeply and personally involved in their work, to feel not only accountable, but engaged.
  • to cultivate depth of character. We want students to develop empathy, imagination, and moral courage; ethical values; a sense of humility; and a habit of service.
  • to help students gain self-knowledge: to learn about their own strengths and weaknesses within a supportive environment that fosters open and realistic communication.
  • to foster a sense of community. Accordingly, we strive to create a rich public life in the school, where students learn to express their opinions effectively and responsibly, and to consider respectfully the opinions of others.

Our Objectives

  • We believe that school is an educational experience. It's where you form your opinions, values, likes and dislikes. School is a second home, and the people in the school, second family. Each one of us is a student on a journey. Each one of us is a teacher, sharing our varied experiences.
  • We believe that each child is unique. At our school, we encourage students to be themselves. Our students are given the space they need to discover, grow, change and experiment.
  • Finally, Education is not making a child ambitious. The child does not have to prove that she has some worth by bringing in gold medals, whether it is in sports, dancing, art or debate. This is our misplaced social conditioning. The ‘Atmopnishad’ says that our internal self is victim of ‘ambition’ and we become vulnerable to baser elements like jealousy, frustration, anger, greed and suicidal feelings.
  • Every child is unique. Let individuality in child grow. Acknowledge the potential within the child and help it to blossom to its full potential. Let the child be herself.
  • Welcome to the journey of expanding and blossoming of our child’s potential.

Arts Education

Always among the highest expression of every culture, the arts teach us much about every historical period through its literature, visual arts, music, dance, and drama. Today it is recognized that to be truly well educated ne must not only learn to appreciate the arts but must have rich opportunities to actively participate in creative work. The arts are languages that most people speak, cutting through individual differences in culture, educational background, and ability.

They can bring every subject to life and turn abstractions into concrete reality. Learning through the arts often results in greater academic achievement and higher test scores. It develops creativity, interpretation skills, critical thinking and communication skills, observation skills and inquisitiveness in children. Cultural Heritage Program

A number of events are celebrated in the school academic year. Annual School Function is conducted where the variety of cultures are explored through performances such as dance, skits and singing songs. School exhibition: ‘Dhamalmasti’ for the prep section where students can have fun with their family and the elder students can exhibit their talents through food items, articles, books, game zone, etc. ‘Mathsomania’ for the students of std. 1st to 10th where they can have special counters to sell the items such as stationary, imitation jewellery, food items, tattoo making, paintings and drawing with special counters such as Maths zone to play Math games, Game zone, Book counters and Selfy zone. TNRSG also celebrates the festivals of India with special days’ celebration such as Republic Day, Independence Day, World Environment Day, Yoga Day, Health Day, Grandparents Day, etc.

Life Skills Program

World Health Organization has defined Life Skills as “the abilities for adaptive and positive behaviour that enable individuals to deal effectively with the demands and challenges of everyday life”. Life Skills educations helps in promotion of mental and social well being and competence in young children to face the realities of life. The ultimate aim of education is the overall development of personality which cannot be achieved without exposing students to various Life Skills. The ten core (generic) Life Skills as listed by WHO are Self Awareness, Empathy, Critical Thinking, Creative Thinking, Decision Making, Problem Solving, Effective Communication, Interpersonal Relationships, Coping with Stress and Managing Emotions. The school Life Skills program in all classes encourages opportunity for building skills and positive habits, recognition of self and ability to think and solve problems.
